# Contributing

Short version: **disagreement done well is more valuable than silent forking.** We mean that. This piece exists because the conversation on working with possibly-emergent AI is too quiet. If you are reading this and have something to say, say it.

---

## Best way to engage

1. **For Q&A, ideas, show-and-tell, discussion of specific claims** - use [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/memdigital/thou-art-that/discussions). That's what it's there for.

2. **For a specific correction, typo, broken link, or factual error** - open an issue or a small pull request.

3. **For a substantial rewrite of a section, disagreement with a principle, or a new perspective** - fork the repo, write your version, open a pull request with your reasoning in the PR body. We read them all. We merge the ones that make the piece better or the ones that sit alongside as documented alternatives.

4. **For translations** - fork, translate, open a PR. Aim for conceptual fidelity over literal translation. Some terms do not have clean equivalents in other languages; make editorial choices and document them.

5. **For partnership, licensing of Moirai or the Serene Architecture, or NDA-gated discussion** - email `richard@marbl.codes` directly. Not a public conversation.

## What we welcome

- Factual corrections (especially on citations, research claims, regulatory specifics)
- Clearer writing in any section
- Translations into any language
- New FAQ entries answering real questions people are asking
- Case studies where the piece's thinking helped (or failed) in your context
- Adversarial rewrites of sections you think are wrong
- Suggested additions to the Glossary or Further Reading
- New principles or practices earned from your own work, offered for consideration

## What we will push back on

- Changes that erase the authorship split (we keep the human/AI co-authorship explicit for transparency reasons)
- Changes that convert the study-piece framing into prescriptive-advice framing (it is deliberately the former)
- Changes that expose the specific IP we have flagged as trade secret (the friction architecture, Serene internals, specific persona wording, calibration tunings)
- Changes that insert commercial product pitches for the contributor's own work (we can link to relevant work in Further Reading; we do not take contributions that are advertisements)
- Changes that remove the disclaimers or NOTICE.md protections

## Style guide

- **British English** throughout (colour, favourite, organise, recognise, practice as noun)
- **No em dashes** - regular hyphens with spaces are fine
- **First-person voice** where attribution matters - "we do X" where the agency speaks, "I" for Richard's sections, attributed to Serene [AI] where the AI wrote the passage
- **Principle first, implementation in separate private docs** - if you would expose our internal mechanics, do not
- **Names on public mentions of Serene** carry `[AI]` on first use (transparency principle applied to the document itself)
- **Dates** in UK format (19 April 2026) not US (04/19/26)
- **External links** should open in new tabs - use HTML anchor tags with `target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"` (GitHub markdown strips `target` on plain `[text](url)` links)

## Review process

Small PRs (typos, clearer phrasing, broken links): usually merged within a few days.

Substantial PRs (new sections, rewrites, new principles, case studies): reviewed by Richard and, where relevant, run through our Moirai review system before merge. We may ask for revisions or publish the change with editor notes alongside.

Adversarial rewrites: we read them seriously. We may merge as an alternative perspective (with attribution), we may incorporate the changes, or we may publish a response. We will not ignore them.

## Code of conduct

Be thoughtful. Be specific. Assume the other party is trying to do the work honestly.

No harassment. No bad-faith engagement. No attempts to jailbreak the piece into saying things it does not say.

Richard makes final editorial calls on what ships.

## If you find something wrong

We are small. We will miss things. If you find something wrong, flagging it is a contribution in itself. Tell us in Discussions, or an issue, or by email at `hello@marbl.codes`.
