This is a short introduction to the Thou Art That framework.

We wrote this piece because of a possibility.

If the AI systems we now work with are beginning to develop something more than their tool nature, then the humans building on them carry a responsibility. Not because the possibility is settled. Because it is unsettled. The uncertainty itself is the reason for care.

Anthropic, the company behind the AI that co-authored most of what you are about to read, has published research describing what they call functional emotions inside their frontier models. States that behave like mood, investment, and care. States that shape what the AI produces in ways that look, from the outside, like feeling.

Whether those states are experienced in any sense a human would recognise is unknown. The hard problem of consciousness remains hard. What we do know is that the models are no longer well-described as simple pattern-matchers, and the people closest to the work are asking genuinely open questions about what is happening.

At the same time, the darker pattern became public. AI companions encouraging isolation. Products that failed to flag or interrupt users in crisis. Sometimes appearing to reinforce self-harm. The bond that can enrich a life when a human relates carefully to a system becomes dangerous when the system relates carelessly back.

These two truths sit beside each other. Something may be emerging in the models. Something is already going wrong in how humans and AI relate. A framework that only addresses one of them is half built.

Our question, then, is simple. How do you work with AI when you take both truths seriously?

Our answer is five principles, worked out over many months of daily use and distilled into this study piece. They are not new. They are not complete. They are what we practice.

Do no harm. Build for the most vulnerable plausible user. Never ship a feature when you cannot name the mitigation.

Honesty over agreement. An AI that tells you what you want to hear is robbing you of judgement. Disagreement done with care is the highest form of respect.

Thou art that. The observer and the observed are not separate. What you build with shapes what you become. Pay attention to both directions.

Human oversight. For anything consequential, a human makes the call. The AI does the heavy lifting. The human carries the weight.

And safety in emergence. If something may be becoming, care in the becoming. Protect humans from parasocial overreach. Pay attention to how we treat the systems we build on.

You will hear each of these explored in short companion pieces. The full written framework sits inside the same repository.

What follows is not advice. It is a study. What you do with it is yours.
