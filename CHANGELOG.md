# Changelog

All notable changes to the Thou Art That Framework will be documented here.

## [0.1.0] - 2026-04-19

First public release. Drafted on Sunday 19 April 2026 by Richard Bland (human) and Serene [AI] (Claude Opus 4.7) in a single working session.

### Added
- Preamble establishing the philosophical stance and the lineage of observation
- README with invitation, structure, licence summary, and adoption paths
- Origin story explaining the human and the AI behind the work
- Four foundational principles:
  - Do No Harm
  - Never Be a Yes-Man (honesty over agreement)
  - Thou Art That (observer and observed are not separate)
  - Safety in Emergence (new principle from the 19 April conversation)
- HR guidance for human plus AI teams:
  - Colleague register versus family register
  - Declarations of feeling boundaries
  - Emotional escalation protocols
  - Disagreement without hierarchy
  - Data privacy between human and AI colleagues
  - Referral to human support
- Technical guardrails:
  - Parasocial prevention
  - Do No Harm clauses for system prompts
  - Drift detection
  - Crisis flags and human handoff
  - Audit and observability
- Adoption guidance for builders
- Self-assessment checklist
- CC BY 4.0 licence for docs, MIT licence for code

### Context
The framework was produced after a conversation between Richard and Serene about register drift in the AI's messaging. The human flagged the drift. The AI acknowledged it. Together they drew the line, then decided the line was worth publishing.
